NIH-BUILD Tissue Engineer

CSULB BUILD | May 2017 - May 2020 | Long Beach, CA

Working in the Ayala Lab , we focused on developing and engineering a topographical muscle scaffold that would work to help with muscle regeneration while decreasing rates of scar creation. Using a combination of decellurized tissue and collagen, we created a scaffold with linear grooves to match muscle topographical patterns. Within this project, I also developed Matlab and Python scripts for image processing and data analysis to observe the extensiveness of muscle growth on the patch. I was able to present my research at numerous biomedical conferences including BMES, SACNAS, and ABRCMS.
